# 🔍 Discovery Meeting Framework

> A practical framework for conducting high-impact discovery meetings that uncover customer needs, build trust, and identify long-term business opportunities.

---
## 🎯 Purpose

The discovery meeting is the foundation of every successful customer relationship.

Its objective is not to sell immediately, but to understand the customer's business, identify opportunities, and build trust.

---

# 🎯 Objectives

During every discovery meeting I aim to:

- Understand the customer's business.
- Identify current challenges.
- Discover business goals.
- Identify decision-makers.
- Understand the buying process.
- Determine whether there is a strategic fit.

---

# 📋 Discovery Checklist

Before every meeting I prepare:

✅ Company research

✅ Industry overview

✅ Customer LinkedIn profile

✅ Previous interactions

✅ CRM notes

✅ Meeting agenda

---

# 💬 Key Questions

## Business

- Tell me about your company.
- What are your main business objectives this year?
- What does success look like for your team?

---

## Challenges

- What are your biggest operational challenges?
- What processes would you like to improve?
- What is currently not working?

---

## Decision Process

- Who is involved in the decision-making process?
- What does your purchasing process look like?
- What timeline are you working with?

---

## Opportunities

- If you could solve one problem today, what would it be?
- What would have the greatest impact on your business?

---

# 🤝 Best Practices

✔ Listen more than you speak.

✔ Ask open-ended questions.

✔ Take structured notes.

✔ Avoid pitching too early.

✔ Focus on understanding before proposing solutions.

✔ Summarize key takeaways before ending the meeting.

---

# 🚀 Expected Outcome

At the end of a successful discovery meeting, I should clearly understand:

- Customer goals
- Pain points
- Decision makers
- Timeline
- Budget indicators
- Potential opportunities
- Next steps
